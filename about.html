Beauty and the Beast is a 4,000-year-old story that has been told many different ways over time. In 1756 Jeanne-Marie Leprince de Beaumont wrote the most widely circulated version of Beauty in the Beast. In his version, Belle is the daughter of a struggling merchant with two sisters who envy her. One day when her father happens upon a castle while traveling he enters it to avoid a storm. Inside the castle, he finds no one home so he eats, warms himself by the fire and sleeps. The morning of his departure he stumbles upon a rose garden and takes a rose for Belle. This action angers the Beast and he only spares the merchants life in exchange for one of his daughter’s becoming the Beast’s prisoner.

Belle agrees to go and is surprised that she is treated to an extravagant lifestyle. She only sees the beast every night at dinner and slowly falls in love with him. However, after a few months, she becomes homesick so the beast allows her to go back home, but he warns her that her trip must be brief because without him he’ll die of a broken heart. The sisters jealous of Belle’s new lavish life convince her to stay longer than she’s supposed to, hoping the beast will eat her out of anger. When Belle does return to the beast she finds him dying of sadness, she realizes she loves him and begs him to marry her. Her desperate pleas transform the beast into a handsome prince and she is made a princess. The End.

Beaumont’s tale steals inspiration from Gabrielle-Suzanne Barbot de Villeneuve’s earlier version which included more fantasy elements like talking monkeys and fairies. This version explains more about the beast's curse, gives Belle a more extensive backstory and the ending is vastly different.

There are different retellings of this story among most culture’s. The Italian version is called “The Pig King”(the mid-1600s) written by Giovanni Francesco Straparola where a prince is born cursed to be a pig until he finds someone who loves him for him. The Norwegian telling is called “East of the Sun, West of the Moon” in which a white bear convinces a peasant to hand over his daughter in marriage. She can’t see him during the day and when she does her husband he is forced to marry someone else. This is also similar to the Greek myth Eros and Psyche.

The most widely known version of beauty and the beast is Disney animated retelling done in 1991. In this story, Belle is known as a bookworm which leaves her an outcast in her village because girls from her “small provincial town” aren’t educated. While still remaining selfless and kind, this Belle was made more independent and less docile than her predecessors. That and other small details are changed in this latest version however, the story remains the same, a beautiful girl falling for an ugly creature despite his looks. Disney recently also released a live action Beauty and the Beast in 2017 that followed the heels of it’s original and added a feminist tweak.
